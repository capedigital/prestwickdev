Diverse Books for Your Classroom

Build richer reading lists with books that reflect our multicultural society.

When it comes to literature, representation goes a long way.

In today’s vibrant society, the importance of diverse and inclusive literature can’t be understated. When students see themselves represented in the literature they read, they’re more likely to engage with the text. Conversely, presenting students with stories that explore experiences unlike their own gives them opportunities to see things from someone else’s perspective.

Over time, exposure to a multitude of cultures, ideas, and backgrounds helps all students develop empathy towards others and expands their understanding of the world around them.
